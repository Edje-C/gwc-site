About Us

Girls Who Code was founded in 2012 by American lawyer Reshma Saujani, who started the club hoping to close the gender gap in the technology industry by introducing coding to adolescent and teenage girls. Besides being Founder and CEO of Girls Who Code, Saujani is an activist andpublic advocate who has inspired over 40,000 girls to learn about tech while realizing that they too can have a tremendous impact on their world both locally and globally.

Girls Who Code @ Newark Public Library has just completed its third year with increasing attendance each succeeding year. During 2016-2017, the girls were visited by guest speakers from Jet.com and traveled to Google Headquarters in New York City. Finally, GWC@ NPL attended a Girls Who Code mini-conference at Prudential in Newark, where they worked with girls from other Girls Who Code clubs throughout northern New Jersey and discussed creating a personal brand, the importance of women in the workforce, equal pay, and other socially relevantissues.
